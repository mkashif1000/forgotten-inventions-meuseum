Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as a "forgotten invention"?

+

We consider any technology or invention that was developed but failed to achieve widespread adoption or was later rendered obsolete. This includes technologies that were ahead of their time, lost format wars, faced insurmountable technical challenges, or were simply outcompeted by alternatives.

Is the Forgotten Inventions Museum a physical location?

+

Currently, we exist solely as a virtual museum. However, we occasionally partner with physical museums and technology exhibitions to showcase forgotten inventions in temporary exhibits. We hope to establish a permanent physical location in the future.

How do you verify the historical accuracy of submissions?

+

Our team of technology historians and researchers reviews all submissions for historical accuracy. We rely on primary sources, patent records, contemporary accounts, and academic research to verify information. If we cannot confirm certain details, we clearly note this in the exhibition.

Can I donate a physical forgotten invention to the museum?

+

While we don't currently maintain a physical collection, we are interested in photographing and documenting physical artifacts. If you have a rare or significant item, please contact us through the submission form, and we can arrange to document it for our virtual collection.

Why study failed or forgotten technologies?

+

Failed technologies provide valuable insights into innovation processes, market dynamics, and technological evolution. Many abandoned ideas contain brilliant solutions that were simply ahead of their time or faced practical limitations that no longer exist. By studying technological "dead ends," we can learn lessons applicable to current and future innovation.

How can I contribute to the museum besides submitting inventions?

+

We welcome various forms of contribution, including:

  • Research assistance and fact-checking
  • Translation of content into other languages
  • Technical expertise on specific technologies
  • Oral history interviews if you were involved with a forgotten technology
  • Financial support through our donation program

Contact us through the submission form if you're interested in contributing in any of these ways.

Do you accept inventions from all time periods?

+

Yes, we accept forgotten inventions from any era, from ancient times to the recent past. We are particularly interested in documenting technologies from the 19th century onward, as this period saw rapid technological advancement and many fascinating alternative paths that weren't taken.

Are you affiliated with any academic institutions?

+

While we maintain collaborative relationships with several university history of technology departments, we are an independent organization. These academic partnerships help ensure the accuracy and scholarly rigor of our exhibitions.

Didn't find your answer?
